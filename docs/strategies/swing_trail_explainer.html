How the bot steps up the Stop Loss using swing pivots — without jumping too early

①Entry
Trade opens. Initial SL placed below entry structure. Bot calls find_swing_high_target() → sets Ref High ① as the next swing high above price.
②Price approaches Ref High ① — SL does NOT move yet
Price rises but has not yet broken Ref High ①. SL stays at original level. No swing low can qualify until the ref is broken.
③Ref High ① broken — waiting for a NEW swing low to form after it
Price closes above Ref High ①. Bot calls find_swing_low_after_ref(). It looks for a pivot low that formed after the Ref High ① candle. None yet → SL still frozen.
④Swing Low A confirmed → SL steps up
Price pulls back, forms a clear pivot low after the Ref High ① candle. Bot sets SL = swing_low_A × (1 − buffer). SL advances only upward. New Ref High ② is set above current price.
⑤Ref High ② broken → waiting again
Price breaks Ref High ②. Same wait — bot won't move SL until a new swing low forms after this candle.
⑥Swing Low B confirmed → SL steps up again
New pivot low forms after Ref High ②. SL advances to swing_low_B × (1 − buffer). Cycle repeats as price continues higher.
